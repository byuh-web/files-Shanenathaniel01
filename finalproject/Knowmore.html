9 Days of Celebration

Bathukamma is celebrated for nine days in nine different ways and each day has a specific meaning and significance as mentioned below.

Day Speciality
Engili Pula Bathukamma The festival begins with offering naivedyam made of sesame seeds and flowers to seek blessings for good health.
Atukula Bathukamma Women offer flattened rice (atukulu) along with flowers to celebrate abundance and nourishment.
Muddapappu Bathukamma Cooked lentils (muddapappu) are offered, symbolizing purity, simplicity, and gratitude
Nanabiyyam Bathukamma Soaked rice is offered to represent prosperity and the importance of food in daily life
Atla Bathukamma Small dosas (atlu) are prepared and offered, marking joy and togetherness in the community
Aligina Bathukamma Aligina Bathukamma marks the day when the Mother Goddess is sorrowful, so she is worshipped tenderly and reverently to lovingly return to her devotees.
Vepakayala Bathukamma Neem fruit and flowers are used, symbolizing protection, healing, and strength
Vennela muddala Bathukamma It signifies purity and prosperity, with the soft white “muddalu” symbolizing peace, harmony, and the gentle glow of moonlight.
Saddula Bathukamma The grand final day when multiple special dishes are offered before immersing Bathukamma in water

  • This is the longes mid term vacation in Telangana State in India.

  • Bathukamma is the official state festival.

  • Bathukamma is celebrated during the Telugu months of Bhadrapadam and Ashwayujam, which correspond to the period between September and October in the Gregorian calendar.

  • This is a frstival that brings families and communities togather and share love towards eachother.
