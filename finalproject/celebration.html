Celebration of Bathukamma Festival

Bathukamma is celebrated with great joy across Telangana, beginning days before the festival as women gather vibrant seasonal flowers like tangedu, gunuka, marigold, and lotus. These flowers are arranged in circular tiers to form the iconic Bathukamma, symbolizing prosperity, nature, and feminine strength. Homes are cleaned, courtyards decorated, and families prepare for evenings filled with songs and gatherings. As part of the preparations, women also make traditional Bathukamma special foods such as pindivantakalu and sathipindi, which hold cultural and nutritional significance. These dishes are prepared with care and shared among family members, adding warmth and tradition to the celebrations. During the festival days, women dress in colorful sarees and young girls in bright langa vonis, carrying their beautifully crafted Bathukammas to open spaces. As the sun sets, they gather in circles around the floral arrangement, singing folk songs that praise Goddess Gauri and celebrate nature’s beauty. The rhythmic clapping, graceful steps, and joyful voices create a lively atmosphere of unity and devotion. These songs, passed down through generations, preserve Telangana’s cultural identity. After the singing, families enjoy festive foods together, including pindivantakalu and sathipindi, which are offered as part of the celebration and symbolize nourishment, purity, and gratitude. On the final day, Saddula Bathukamma, the festivities reach their peak. Women carry their Bathukammas in a vibrant procession toward lakes, ponds, or rivers, singing and celebrating along the way. Once they reach the water, the Bathukammas are gently set afloat, symbolizing the goddess’s return to her divine home and the community’s hope for blessings in the coming year. The immersion also represents renewal and the letting go of past worries. Families then return home to share festive meals, exchange good wishes, and enjoy traditional dishes like pindivantakalu, sathipindi, and other homemade snacks. This blend of devotion, culture, and togetherness makes Bathukamma a cherished celebration across Telangana.
